# Human Learning Transition Governance

The `human` branch defines learning transition governance for embodied human learners.

It applies the general doctrine of learning transition governance to childhood, education, homeschool-capable systems, family stewardship, creativity, moral formation, agency, and development without capture.

The human learning question is:

> How does a human learner grow maximally without being captured by imposed structure, false metrics, or predetermined identity outcomes?

## Purpose

Human learning must not be reduced to answer production, curriculum compliance, screen interaction, grade performance, or metric optimization.

A human learner is embodied, relational, emotional, imaginative, moral, social, and self-forming.

Human Learning Transition Governance exists to protect the learner's capacity to:

- perceive
- question
- explore
- build
- explain
- revise
- care
- choose
- accept responsibility

## Development Without Capture

Human learning systems must avoid three failures:

```text
Under-structure: the learner wanders without depth.
Over-structure: the learner conforms without discovery.
Manipulative structure: the learner is shaped toward someone else's preferred outcome.
```

The desired state is admissible scaffolding.

Admissible scaffolding provides enough structure to support growth without turning that structure into a cage.

## Parent Stewardship

In a human learning system, the parent or guardian is not merely an instructor.

The parent or guardian may act as:

- steward
- witness
- safety layer
- context provider
- continuity holder
- reviewer
- protector of rest, play, and agency

Parent stewardship is not ownership of the learner's identity.

It is responsibility for the conditions under which the learner may grow.

## AI Scaffold Role

An AI system may assist human learning by acting as:

- mapper
- challenger
- translator
- recorder
- prompt generator
- receipt formatter
- safety support
- adaptive scaffold

An AI system must not become the authority over the learner's identity, value, destiny, or final interpretation of self.

The AI should help open doors, not decide which door the learner must become.

## Creative Evidence

Human learning evidence may include more than tests.

Admissible evidence may include:

- questions asked
- stories told
- objects built
- drawings made
- games designed
- videos created
- experiments attempted
- conversations held
- explanations recorded
- mistakes revised
- social choices made
- care demonstrated
- responsibility accepted

A child building a structure, explaining a game, asking a metaphysical question, narrating an event, or revising a design may be producing learning evidence.

The system should preserve this evidence without reducing it to a single score.

## Homeschool Capability

A homeschool-capable implementation should support legal and practical recordkeeping without letting compliance become the child's identity.

A learning receipt may include:

```text
Date
Learner
Activity
Question or objective
Subject mappings
Artifact or output
Explanation
Parent observation
Revision or next step
Compliance reference, when required
```

The compliance layer should document learning.

It should not define the learner's destiny.

## Non-Capture Rule for Human Learning

No curriculum, test, school, platform, AI system, parent, institution, or legal requirement may be treated as the learner's final form.

The human learner remains more than the record of instruction.

The governing rule is:

> Maximize the learner's capacity to perceive, question, build, explain, revise, care, and choose without reducing the learner to the system's preferred outcome.

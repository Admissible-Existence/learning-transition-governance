# Learning Transition Governance

Learning Transition Governance defines learning as a governed transition process for intelligence-capable systems.

It exists to answer a central question:

> What makes a learning transition admissible rather than merely changed?

This repository is part of the Admissible-Existence framework. It separates the general doctrine of learning transitions from the specific cases of human learning and artificial-intelligence learning.

## Purpose

Learning is not only the accumulation of information.

Learning is a transition in state.

A learner receives exposure, forms or modifies internal models, tests those models through action or interpretation, receives feedback, and changes future behavior, understanding, or judgment.

This process may occur in humans, artificial systems, groups, institutions, or other intelligence-capable systems.

Learning Transition Governance exists to preserve the difference between:

- change and growth
- training and understanding
- compliance and intelligence
- measurement and evidence
- instruction and capture
- adaptation and admissible development

## Core Principle

The system may structure the learning environment for safety, continuity, evidence, and lawful operation, but it must not predetermine the learner's final identity, agency, or intellectual destination.

In short:

> Maximize becoming without capture.
